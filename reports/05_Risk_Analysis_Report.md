# Risk Analysis Report
## End-to-End Banking Data Analytics using PostgreSQL

**Project Version:** 1.0.0

---

# Executive Summary

This report analyzes customer risk categories within the banking dataset to support financial risk management and regulatory reporting.

Risk analysis enables financial institutions to identify customer risk profiles, monitor portfolio exposure, and support informed lending and compliance decisions.

---

# Dataset Summary

| Metric | Value |
|---------|-------:|
| Total Records | 24,997 |
| Unique Customers | 11,676 |
| Risk Categories | 4 |

---

# Risk Category Distribution

| Risk Category | Customers |
|---------------|----------:|
| Unknown | 8,334 |
| High | 6,689 |
| Medium | 6,623 |
| Low | 3,351 |

### Business Insight

The **Unknown** category contains the largest number of records, indicating an opportunity to improve customer risk assessment and data quality.

---

# Risk Classification Overview

Customers are classified into four categories:

- Low Risk
- Medium Risk
- High Risk
- Unknown

These classifications help financial institutions evaluate lending decisions, monitor compliance requirements, and prioritize customer reviews.

---

# Transaction Activity by Risk Category

The banking dataset supports analysis of transaction values across different customer risk profiles.

Key observations include:

- High-risk customers contribute significantly to overall transaction activity.
- Medium-risk customers represent a balanced portion of the customer base.
- Low-risk customers form the smallest group.
- Unknown classifications reduce the effectiveness of portfolio risk analysis.

---

# Business Impact

Customer risk classifications support:

- Credit decision-making
- AML and KYC monitoring
- Customer due diligence
- Portfolio risk assessment
- Regulatory compliance
- Executive reporting

---

# Data Quality Observations

Several customer records remain classified as **Unknown**.

Possible causes include:

- Missing source information
- Incomplete customer profiles
- Inconsistent classification during data collection
- Historical records without updated risk assessments

---

# Key Findings

- Four customer risk categories are represented.
- Unknown classifications account for the largest segment.
- High-risk customers form a substantial portion of the portfolio.
- Improved classification would increase reporting accuracy.
- Risk categorization enables more effective portfolio monitoring.

---

# Recommendations

To improve risk management:

- Reduce the number of Unknown classifications.
- Perform regular customer risk reassessments.
- Strengthen KYC data collection.
- Automate customer risk classification where appropriate.
- Monitor changes in customer risk over time.

---

# Business Value

Accurate risk analysis enables organizations to:

- Improve lending quality
- Reduce financial risk
- Support regulatory compliance
- Enhance customer segmentation
- Improve executive reporting
- Strengthen strategic decision-making

---

# Conclusion

The banking dataset provides valuable information for customer risk analysis and portfolio monitoring.

Although most customer records are successfully classified, reducing the number of Unknown risk categories would significantly improve reporting quality and support more informed business decisions.
